KEY CHANGES FROM FY2025
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Singapore Ramp-Up: Budget reflects full-year operation of Singapore office
   including 4 FTE (2 custody, 1 compliance, 1 business development), office
   lease, MAS compliance costs, and local legal/accounting. This is the single
   largest incremental increase.

2. SSN Platform: R&D increase of NZD 97K supports Phase 3 development (SaaS
   multi-tenant architecture, API marketplace). Henry has committed to
   delivering beta by Q2 FY2026.

3. Staff Growth: +6 FTE across Wellington (2), Auckland (1), Sydney (2),
   Singapore (1). Includes new Relationship Manager for APAC enterprise sales.

4. Compliance: ISO 27001 recertification, SOC 2 Type II renewal, and new
   Singapore PDPA/MAS compliance program.


AREAS OF CONCERN
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Singapore Ramp-Up Costs: We are budgeting for NZD 420K in Singapore
   operations before meaningful revenue. Pipeline suggests first AU/SG
   contracts may not close until Q2-Q3 FY2026. Cash flow monitoring essential.
   Recommend monthly variance review for Singapore P&L.

2. SSN Platform R&D Increase: 25% increase is material. Henry has provided
   detailed roadmap; I have requested quarterly milestone gates. Any scope
   creep must be approved via change request.

3. Revenue Assumptions: Budget assumes 18% revenue growth (NZD 5.2M target).
   Pipeline is strong but conversion timing uncertain. Q1 and Q2 will be
   critical for determining whether we need to implement cost containment.


APPROVAL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
FY2026 budget is approved effective 1 April 2026. All department heads have
been notified of their allocations. Quarterly reforecast process remains in
place; material variances (>10%) require CFO and CEO sign-off for revision.
