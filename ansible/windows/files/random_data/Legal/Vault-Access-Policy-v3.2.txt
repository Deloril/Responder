1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This policy defines who may authorise vault access, dual-control requirements,
logging, and emergency procedures for TSG's digital escrow vaults. It supports
compliance with ISO 27001 Annex A controls (A.5.24, A.5.25, A.8.5) and SOC 2
Trust Services Criteria for Security and Availability.


2. AUTHORISATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.1 Primary Authoriser: The Chief Custodian Officer (CCO), Henry Ngata, is
    the sole authority for routine vault access authorisation. No access may
    occur without CCO approval except under Emergency Access (Clause 6).

2.2 Delegation: The CCO may delegate authorisation authority in writing to one
    designated deputy (currently: Sophie Patel). Delegation is limited to
    specific access types and must be documented. Delegation lapses automatically
    if the CCO is unavailable for more than 14 consecutive days without prior
    written extension.

2.3 Client-Initiated Access: Release of escrow materials to a Beneficiary
    requires: (a) verified Release Condition per the escrow agreement;
    (b) written instruction from authorised client representatives; and
    (c) CCO approval. Dual-control applies to all releases.


3. DUAL-CONTROL REQUIREMENTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.1 All vault access (read, write, or release) requires the presence and
    concurrent action of two authorised individuals:

    - Custodian 1: CCO or designated deputy (authoriser)
    - Custodian 2: A second approved custodian from the roster (Ben, Lisa Liu,
      David Nair, or Priya Rangi)

3.2 No single individual may complete a vault transaction. Both custodians must
    authenticate independently (MFA + biometric or hardware token) and log the
    access event.

3.3 The custodian roster is maintained by the CCO and reviewed quarterly.
    Custodians must complete annual security training and pass background
    checks per HR policy.


4. LOGGING AND MONITORING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.1 All vault access events are logged with: timestamp, user identity, action
    type, asset identifier, and outcome. Logs are retained for 7 years.

4.2 Logs are stored in a segregated SIEM (TSG-INTERNAL.LAB\vault-audit) with
    restricted access. Jennifer Li (IT Security) reviews access logs weekly for
    anomalies.

4.3 Unauthorised access attempts trigger immediate alert to CCO and IT Security.
    Three failed authentication attempts within 15 minutes results in temporary
    account lockout and manual review.


5. REVIEW CYCLES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.1 Access Rights Review: Quarterly review of all individuals with vault access
    rights. Removed promptly upon role change or termination.

5.2 Policy Review: This policy is reviewed annually by the CCO and Legal
    (Sarah Wang). Changes require CEO approval.

5.3 Control Testing: Internal Audit (or external auditor) tests dual-control
    and logging effectiveness annually as part of ISO 27001 surveillance.


6. EMERGENCY ACCESS PROCEDURES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.1 Emergency access is permitted only when: (a) the CCO is incapacitated or
    unreachable; (b) a court order or regulatory directive requires immediate
    action; or (c) a critical client Release Condition has occurred and delay
    would cause material harm.

6.2 Emergency authorisation chain: CEO (Daniel Kemp) > COO (Kate) > Legal
    (Sarah Wang). The authorising executive must document the emergency and
    notify the CCO as soon as practicable.

6.3 Emergency access still requires dual-control. The authorising executive
    may designate the second custodian if the usual roster is unavailable.

6.4 All emergency access is logged and reported to the Audit Committee at the
    next meeting. Post-incident review is mandatory.


7. PHYSICAL AND LOGICAL SECURITY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7.1 Vault infrastructure is housed in a Tier III data centre with 24/7
    monitoring. Logical access is protected by AES-256 encryption at rest,
    TLS 1.3 in transit, and hardware security modules (HSMs) for key management.

7.2 Vault keys are split using Shamir's Secret Sharing. No single individual
    holds a complete key. Recovery procedures are documented in a separate
    Business Continuity Plan.
