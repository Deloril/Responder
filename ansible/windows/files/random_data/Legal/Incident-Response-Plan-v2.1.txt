This Incident Response Plan (IRP) establishes procedures for detecting,
containing, eradicating, and recovering from security incidents affecting
TSG systems, vault infrastructure, or client data. Compliance: ISO 27001 A.5.24,
A.5.26; SOC 2 CC7.2; Privacy Act 2020.

================================================================================

================================================================================
2. SEVERITY LEVELS
================================================================================

SEV-1 (Critical): Active breach of vault or client data; ransomware; sustained
    outage of primary systems. Full IRT activation. CEO/COO notified within
    30 minutes.

SEV-2 (High): Suspected unauthorised access; malware detected; partial outage.
    IRT activation. COO notified within 2 hours.

SEV-3 (Medium): Policy violation; failed intrusion attempt; single system
    compromise. Limited IRT. CISO notified within 4 hours.

SEV-4 (Low): Phishing attempt; minor policy violation; isolated anomaly.
    Logged and triaged. Weekly review.

================================================================================
3. RESPONSE PROCEDURES
================================================================================

3.1 Detection & Triage (0-1 hour)
    - SIEM/CrowdStrike alert or staff report received
    - Technical Lead performs initial assessment
    - IC declares severity and activates appropriate response level
    - Incident ticket created in ServiceNow (TSG-IR-YYYY-NNNN)

3.2 Containment (1-4 hours)
    - Isolate affected systems; preserve evidence
    - Revoke compromised credentials; block malicious IPs
    - For vault incidents: Chief Custodian Officer (Henry Ngata) consulted
      for dual-control authorisation of any emergency access restrictions

3.3 Eradication & Recovery
    - Remove threat; rebuild from clean backups
    - Password resets; certificate rotation as needed
    - Validate integrity before restoring to production

3.4 Post-Incident
    - Root cause analysis within 5 business days
    - Lessons learned documented; IRP updated
    - Annual tabletop exercise (next: June 2025)

================================================================================
4. NOTIFICATION TIMELINES - PRIVACY ACT 2020 (NZ)
================================================================================

4.1 Notifiable Privacy Breach (s 117)
    Where unauthorised access to personal information has occurred and
    serious harm is likely:
    - Notify Office of the Privacy Commissioner: AS SOON AS PRACTICABLE
      (guidance: within 72 hours of becoming aware)
    - Notify affected individuals: AS SOON AS PRACTICABLE
    - Document: Breach register, PIA addendum, notification evidence

4.2 Cross-Border Considerations
    - Australian clients: Notify OAIC if Australian Privacy Act applies
    - Singapore clients: Assess PDPA notification obligations
    - Sarah Wang determines jurisdiction-specific requirements

4.3 Regulator Contact Details
    - Privacy Commissioner NZ: 0800 803 909 | privacy.org.nz
    - CERT NZ: 0800 287 364 | cert.govt.nz
    - FMA (if financial sector): 0800 434 566
