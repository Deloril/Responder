EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
-----------------
This feasibility study evaluates two options for expanding TSG's secure vault
capacity to meet projected growth through 2028: (A) Wellington vault upgrade
and (B) new vault establishment in Sydney. Based on cost analysis, regulatory
considerations, and capacity projections, we recommend Option B - Sydney new
vault - as the preferred approach.

BUSINESS CONTEXT
----------------
Current vault utilisation: 78% (Wellington primary)
Projected growth: 22% CAGR based on pipeline and market analysis
Peak capacity required: 2028 - 2.4x current capacity

OPTION A: WELLINGTON VAULT UPGRADE
----------------------------------

Description:
- Expansion of existing Wellington data centre vault facility
- Additional 120 rack units, upgraded cooling, expanded secure storage
- Same site, enhanced physical security perimeter

Cost Analysis (NZD):
| Item                    | Amount      |
|-------------------------|-------------|
| Physical expansion      | 420,000     |
| Cooling upgrade         | 185,000     |
| Security enhancements   | 95,000      |
| Network infrastructure  | 65,000      |
| Contingency (15%)       | 115,000     |
| TOTAL                   | 880,000     |

Capacity: +85% over current
Timeline: 4-5 months
Regulatory: No new approvals required (existing certifications apply)

OPTION B: SYDNEY NEW VAULT
--------------------------

Description:
- Greenfield vault establishment in Sydney CBD or North Sydney
- Purpose-built facility, 200 rack units initial capacity
- Designed for Australian Financial Services (AFS) compliance from outset

Cost Analysis (AUD):
| Item                    | Amount      |
|-------------------------|-------------|
| Site fit-out            | 680,000     |
| Security (ASIO T4 equiv) | 220,000     |
| Network (redundant)      | 95,000      |
| Regulatory/legal         | 85,000      |
| Contingency (15%)        | 162,000     |
| TOTAL                   | 1,242,000   |
| (NZD equivalent ~1.35M)  |             |

Capacity: New 200 RU + Wellington retained = 2.2x total capacity
Timeline: 6-7 months
Regulatory: AFS licence considerations (see below)

REGULATORY CONSIDERATIONS - AUSTRALIAN FINANCIAL SERVICES
---------------------------------------------------------
- Escrow services may require AFSL if deemed "financial product" - legal
  opinion sought: low risk for pure custodianship, but documentation required
- APRA-regulated clients may require Australian domicile for data
- Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) - Australian Privacy Principles apply
- Data sovereignty: Australian client data must remain in Australia
- Recommendation: Engage Australian legal counsel (SGD office project) for
  definitive AFS requirements; budget AUD 25K for compliance review

CAPACITY PROJECTIONS
--------------------
| Year  | Wellington Only | Wellington + Sydney |
|-------|-----------------|---------------------|
| 2026  | 92%             | 65% / 45%           |
| 2027  | 118% (exceeded) | 78% / 62%           |
| 2028  | 145% (exceeded) | 88% / 75%           |

Wellington upgrade alone reaches capacity by mid-2027. Sydney option provides
headroom through 2029 and enables Australian market expansion.

RECOMMENDATION
--------------
Option B (Sydney new vault) is recommended for the following reasons:

1. Strategic: Aligns with Singapore office expansion; creates Asia-Pacific
   hub (Sydney + Singapore) for redundancy and client preference
2. Regulatory: Proactively addresses Australian data sovereignty requirements
   from financial services clients
3. Capacity: Superior long-term capacity; Wellington upgrade is stopgap
4. Risk: Geographic diversification reduces single-point-of-failure
5. Cost: Higher upfront but better unit economics at scale; Wellington
   upgrade would require Sydney investment within 18-24 months regardless

CONDITIONAL APPROVAL SOUGHT
---------------------------
- Proceed with Option B subject to: (a) AFS legal clearance, (b) Board
  approval of AUD 1.24M budget, (c) Sydney site selection by 28 Feb 2026
