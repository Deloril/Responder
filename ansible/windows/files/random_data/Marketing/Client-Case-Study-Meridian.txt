CLIENT PROFILE
--------------
Company: Meridian Financial Group
Industry: Financial Services (Asset Management)
Headquarters: Auckland, New Zealand
AUM: NZD 4.2 billion
Employees: 180

THE CHALLENGE
-------------
Meridian Financial Group relied on a third-party portfolio management platform
for their core investment operations. The vendor, a mid-sized software company,
held exclusive custody of the source code. Meridian's board identified critical
vendor concentration risk: in the event of vendor insolvency, acquisition, or
dispute, they would lose access to the system underpinning their entire
investment process.

Regulatory pressure compounded the issue. Reserve Bank of New Zealand
guidelines and FMA expectations required documented continuity arrangements
for material outsourced systems. Meridian's auditors had flagged the absence
of escrow as a control deficiency for two consecutive years.

"Our board was uncomfortable with the exposure. We had no leverage, no
visibility, and no fallback if things went wrong." - Meridian CTO

THE SOLUTION
------------
TSG designed a three-party escrow arrangement involving Meridian (licensee),
the software vendor (depositor), and TSG (escrow agent). Key elements:

• Source Code Escrow: Full repository including build scripts, dependencies,
  and documentation deposited quarterly with TSG's Wellington vault
• Verification Events: Annual independent verification by TSG to confirm
  completeness and buildability of deposited materials
• Release Conditions: Clear triggers (insolvency, material breach, failure
  to support) with 30-day cure period and independent arbitration provision
• Multi-Region Backup: Replicated custody in Sydney for business continuity

TSG's SSN platform provided Meridian with real-time deposit status visibility
and audit trails, satisfying their compliance requirements. The arrangement
was negotiated within 90 days with full vendor cooperation.

THE RESULTS
-----------
• Vendor risk reduced: Meridian now has contractual assurance of source code
  access under defined release conditions
• Audit finding resolved: FMA and external auditors accepted the arrangement
  as adequate control for outsourced system continuity
• Board confidence: Escrow became a template for other critical vendor
  relationships
• Implementation timeline: 90 days from engagement to first deposit

"TSG understood our regulatory context immediately. They didn't try to sell us
a standard product - they worked with our vendor to design something that
worked for everyone. The verification process gave us real confidence."
- Sarah Chen, Chief Risk Officer, Meridian Financial Group

LESSONS LEARNT
--------------
• Early vendor engagement accelerates implementation
• Verification frequency should match system criticality
• Clear release conditions prevent disputes
• Multi-region custody addresses business continuity concerns
