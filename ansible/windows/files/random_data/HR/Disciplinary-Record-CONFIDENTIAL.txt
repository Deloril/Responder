RECORD 1: UNAUTHORISED ACCESS TO VAULT RECORDS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Case ID:           DISC-2025-007
Employee:          David Nair
Employee ID:       TSG-2019-042
Department:        Finance
Position:          Accountant
Manager:           Lisa Liu, Finance Director

Date of Incident:  14 August 2025
Date Reported:     16 August 2025
Reported By:       System alert (Vault Access Log) + Wiremu Sio, Head of Vault Ops

Summary:
On 14 August 2025, David Nair accessed vault metadata records for Client
Account #7842 (identity redacted) via the internal vault management portal.
Mr Nair's role does not require vault record access; his access rights are
limited to financial reconciliation data only. The access occurred outside
business hours (22:34 NZST) from his work-issued device. No data was
downloaded or exported. System logs confirmed single query access.

Investigation:
- Interview with David Nair conducted 18 August 2025 (Kate, Ben present)
- Mr Nair stated he was "checking something" related to a billing query and
  had "clicked through" to the wrong screen. He acknowledged he had not
  completed the required Vault Access Procedures training refresh (due
  March 2025).
- IT (Oliver Anderson) confirmed no evidence of data exfiltration.
- Sophie Patel (Legal) advised: policy breach, no client complaint, no
  regulatory notification required.

Outcome:
- Formal Written Warning issued 22 August 2025
- Mandatory re-completion of Vault Security Procedures training
- Access rights reviewed and restricted; vault portal access revoked
- Warning to remain on file for 12 months
- No further action if no repeat within 12 months

Employee Acknowledgment: Signed 22 August 2025
Appeal: None lodged

RECORD 2: REPEATED LATE TIMEKEEPING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Case ID:           DISC-2025-012
Employee:          Karen Tavita
Employee ID:       TSG-2021-089
Department:        Vault Operations
Position:          Vault Coordinator
Manager:           Wiremu Sio, Head of Vault Operations

Date of Incidents: Multiple – June to September 2025
Date of Meeting:   2 October 2025
Reported By:       Wiremu Sio (manager) following informal discussions

Summary:
Karen Tavita accumulated 23 late arrivals (defined as >15 minutes after
scheduled start) between 1 June and 30 September 2025. Vault Operations
requires punctuality due to shift handover and security procedures. Ms
Tavita's scheduled start is 08:00; late arrivals ranged from 08:16 to 09:45.
No pattern (e.g. specific days) was identified. Ms Tavita had received
informal verbal feedback from Wiremu Sio in July and August 2025. A formal
discussion was triggered when late arrivals continued into September.

Investigation:
- Meeting held 2 October 2025: Kate (HR), Wiremu Sio, Karen Tavita
- Ms Tavita cited childcare drop-off and traffic as contributing factors.
  She acknowledged the impact on team handover and agreed to explore
  flexible start (08:15) with manager approval.
- Wiremu Sio agreed to trial 08:15 start for 3 months, subject to coverage.

Outcome:
- First Written Warning issued 5 October 2025
- Flexible start 08:15 approved until 5 January 2026; review at that time
- Warning to remain on file for 6 months
- If late arrivals exceed 3 in any month during warning period, escalation
  to Final Written Warning

Employee Acknowledgment: Signed 5 October 2025
Appeal: None lodged

RECORD 3: INAPPROPRIATE USE OF COMPANY EMAIL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Case ID:           DISC-2025-015
Employee:          Arjun Jones
Employee ID:       TSG-2020-067
Department:        Sales & Client Relations
Position:          Client Relationship Manager
Manager:           Liam Jones, Head of Sales & Client Relations

Date of Incident:  Multiple – July to October 2025
Date Reported:     4 November 2025
Reported By:       IT Security (Ava Phan) – automated policy flag

Summary:
Automated monitoring detected use of company email (arjun.jones@tsg-internal.lab)
for personal purposes in breach of the Acceptable Use Policy. Analysis of
flagged traffic (sample) indicated:
- Forwarding of non-work-related content (chain emails, promotional material)
  to personal email addresses
- Use of work email for personal online service registrations (e.g. retail,
  entertainment)
- Volume of personal-use traffic estimated at 15–20% of total sent traffic
  during the period

No client data, confidential information, or vault-related content was
involved. The breach was policy-based rather than security-compromise.

Investigation:
- Meeting held 8 November 2025: Kate (HR), Liam Jones, Arjun Jones
- Mr Jones acknowledged he had used work email for convenience and was
  unaware of the extent of the policy. He expressed remorse and agreed
  to comply.
- Ava Phan confirmed no data loss or external exposure.

Outcome:
- Formal Written Warning issued 12 November 2025
- Mandatory completion of Acceptable Use Policy e-learning (within 7 days)
- Warning to remain on file for 12 months
- Reminder that company systems are monitored and further breaches may
  result in disciplinary action up to and including termination

Employee Acknowledgment: Signed 12 November 2025
Appeal: None lodged
