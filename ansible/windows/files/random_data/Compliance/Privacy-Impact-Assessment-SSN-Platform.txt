EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
-----------------
This Privacy Impact Assessment evaluates the Secure Secrets Network (SSN) platform
under the Privacy Act 2020. The SSN platform enables clients to deposit, manage,
and release digital assets and confidential materials through TSG's vault
infrastructure. This PIA identifies data flows, privacy risks, and controls to
ensure lawful processing of personal information.


1. PROJECT OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The SSN platform is a Phase 2 development initiative enabling API-based escrow
deposits, automated release workflows, and client self-service portals. The
platform processes personal data of client contacts, authorized signatories, and
vault access delegates across TSG offices (Wellington, Auckland, Christchurch,
Sydney, Melbourne, Singapore).


2. DATA FLOWS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.1 Data Collected
   - Client contact details (name, email, phone, role)
   - Authorized signatory identities and verification documents
   - Vault access logs (user ID, timestamp, action, IP address)
   - KYC/AML verification data for high-value escrow clients
   - Support ticket correspondence and call recordings

2.2 Data Sources
   - Direct client input via SSN portal
   - Manual entry by TSG staff (Lisa Liu, Priya Rangi)
   - Integration with Meridian Financial Group and other escrow clients
   - Identity verification providers (third-party)

2.3 Data Recipients
   - TSG internal staff with role-based access
   - Clients (their own data only)
   - Regulators (upon lawful request)
   - Sub-processors: AWS (APAC region), Datacom managed services


3. PRIVACY RISKS IDENTIFIED
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Risk ID    Description                          Likelihood  Impact   Inherent
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PIA-01     Unauthorized access to client PII     Medium      High     High
PIA-02     Data retention beyond lawful period   Low         Medium   Medium
PIA-03     Cross-border transfer to Singapore   Medium      Medium   Medium
PIA-04     Insufficient consent for marketing    Low         Low      Low
PIA-05     Vault access logs identify individuals Medium     High     High
PIA-06     Inadequate breach notification       Low         High     Medium
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------


4. CONTROLS IMPLEMENTED
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.1 Technical Controls
   - Encryption at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3)
   - Role-based access control (RBAC) - Chief Custodian Officer approval
   - Audit logging of all PII access (ISO 27001 A.12.4.1)
   - Data minimisation in API responses
   - Pseudonymisation of access logs after 90 days

4.2 Organisational Controls
   - Privacy training for SSN platform team (Jennifer Li, Ben)
   - Data Processing Agreements with sub-processors
   - Privacy Notice published at ssn.tsg.co.nz/privacy
   - Designated Privacy Officer: David Nair

4.3 Cross-Border Transfer
   - Singapore office: Standard Contractual Clauses, PDPA alignment
   - Australian offices: Australian Privacy Principles compliance
   - Data residency: NZ primary, Singapore DR only for technical failover

6. DPIA OUTCOMES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.1 High-Risk Activities
   - Processing of KYC/AML data: Mitigated via access controls, encryption
   - Cross-border transfer to Singapore: Mitigated via SCCs, PDPA review

6.2 Consultation
   - Office of the Privacy Commissioner: Pre-implementation consultation
   - Internal: Sophie Patel (Compliance), Henry Ngata (CCO)

6.3 Recommendations
   - Implement automated data retention purge (Q1 2026)
   - Conduct PIA review upon SSN Phase 3 (biometric auth) - deferred
   - Document data flow diagrams in Confluence (TSG-INTERNAL.LAB)

6.4 Sign-Off
   - DPIA outcome: Proceed with implementation, subject to controls
   - Residual risk: Low (with controls)
   - Next review: August 2026 or upon material change
